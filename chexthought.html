Abstract

Chest X-ray (CXR) interpretation is among the most frequently performed diagnostic imaging tasks worldwide, yet existing datasets capture only final reports or image-level labels, omitting the cognitive processes underlying expert diagnosis. To address this gap, we introduce CheXthought, the first large-scale, globally distributed dataset integrating human chain-of-thought reasoning with synchronized visual attention for CXR interpretation. CheXthought comprises 103,592 reasoning traces and 6,609,082 spatial annotations across 50,312 multi-read chest X-rays, contributed by 501 radiologists spanning 71 countries.


In blinded expert evaluation, CheXthought chains-of-thought significantly outperform those generated by GPT 5.2, Claude Opus 4.5, and MedGemma 1 across comprehensiveness, factuality, and spatial localization. Fine-tuning vision–language models on CheXthought yields substantial improvements in pathology classification, achieving 65% accuracy on external datasets compared to 46% with report-based supervision and 34.5% with synthetic reasoning. At inference, incorporating visual attention data from CheXthought further improves performance, recovering missed findings in 84% of cases for MedGemma and 77% for Qwen-8B-Thinking, while significantly reducing hallucinations.


Leveraging CheXthought’s multi-reader annotations, we train a model to predict inter-reader disagreement as a proxy for diagnostic uncertainty and case difficulty. This model demonstrates strong calibration to human variability, with 92.5% of predictions falling within ±10 percentage points of ground-truth disagreement percent. Together, these results establish CheXthought as a reference for visually grounded clinical reasoning, enabling the development and benchmarking of more reliable, interpretable, and uncertainty-aware medical AI systems.

Dataset Construction

Data Source

50,312 chest X-rays are sourced from the CheXpert Plus dataset.

Data Extraction

Patient information and radiology reports are extracted and linked to the image.

Annotation Platform

Images, reports, and patient data are hosted on a web-based platform capable of displaying DICOM, capturing dictation and spatial annotations.

Global Recruitment

Annotators ranging from PGY-2 trainees to attending radiologists were recruited globally.
